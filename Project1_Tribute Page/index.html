Mahatma Gandhi

1869-1948

"Non-violence is the greatest force at the disposal of mankind."

Mahatma Gandhi's timeline:

  • 1869: Born in India, with the birth name of Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi
  • 1883: Gandhi (13 years-old) was married to 14-year-old Kasturbai Makhanji Kapadia
  • 1888: He enrolled at Samaldas College in Bhavnagar State, but dropped out later. In this same year Gandhi enrolled in the University College, London to study law and jurisprudence. Before going to london he made a vow in front of his mother that he would abstain from meat, alcohol and women.
  • 1891: Gandhi was called to the bar and went back to India to start his lawyer career.
  • 1893: He moved to South Africa to work as a lawyer, where he spent 21 years and developed his political views, ethics and politics.
  • 1905: Gandhi wrote important contributions to the journal column "Indian Opinion"
  • 1908: He worte the book " Hind Swaraj" where he exposed his ideas about Western civilization and the forms of peaceful resistance.
  • 1914: He left South Africa to India, where he disseminate the concept of “Satyagraha” (pacific protest) and became know for the name Mahatma, meaning great soul
  • 1920 - 1940: Gandhi promoted several actions of civil disobedience against the English and was detained several times. Exemples of these acts are the Salt Satyagraha (Salt March) and the boycott of foreign-made goods, especially British goods.
  • 1924 - 1925: After the end of the World War I, the Indian National Congress Party was formed under the leadership of Mahatma Gandhi and Jawaharlal Nahru.The goal was to achieve full independence from India and socio-economic reform, establishing political equality for all.
  • 1947: Indian freedom after great influency of Mahatma Gandhi
  • 1948: The activist suffered an attack in New Delhi and was fatally hit by shots fired by Nathuram Godse, a radical Hindu who held Gandhi responsible for the separation of India and Pakistan.
